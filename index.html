Crowdsourcing

  • Crowdsourcing is evolving as a distributed problem-solving and production model.
  • Crowdsourcing utilize “Human Computation” powers to perform computational tasks that are difficult for computers to process.
It first introduced in 2006 obtain large application in industrial area. Then Crowdsourcing also has the potential to be a problem-solving mechanism for government and nonprofit use. Urban and transit planning are prime areas for crowdsourcing. Researchers have used crowdsourcing systems (in particular, the Mechanical Turk) to aid with research projects by crowdsourcing some aspects of the research process, such as data collection, parsing, and evaluation.  Artists have also utilized crowdsourcing systems. To leverage the creativity of peoples. Image tagging, translation, logo design one of the most import application is data integration.

Data Integration

  • Data integration involves combining data residing in different sources.
  • The age of big data lead to a rapid increase of number of available datasets and they are often fragmented into different sources.

Motivation

  • Preserving all the data would complicates the query processing and increases storage cost.
  • Quality: Removing the uncertainty may cause information lost.

Schema Matching

  • Schema matching is a central challenge for data integration systems.
  • Schema Matching refers to finding correspondences between elements of two given schemas.
  • Schema matching is recognized to be one of the basic operations required by many applications
A large amount of techniques is proposed to address this problem. It is still very difficult to tackle the problem completely by an algorithmic approach. Uncertainty is inherent. Sematic ambiguity.
